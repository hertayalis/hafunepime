The 13 Best Faux Leather Pants of 2024

As much as we love our trusted jeans, there’s just something special about a great pair of leather pants. It’s the style we opt for when we need a little more than a pair of jeans but less than a skirt. Not to mention, there’s an undeniable element of edginess that comes with wearing leather, so naturally, we went on a mission to pick the best ones. In an effort to provide an affordable shopping selection (a pair of genuine leather pants will most often run you over $1,000), we went the vegan route and found the best faux leather pants that are both polished and cool.

Today's Best Deals

As you'll see below, faux leather pants are offered in a variety of styles, colors, finishes, and rises to enhance their appeal. In terms of appearance, it's come a long way, too, looking more and more like the real thing with every season that passes. Now, it's easier than ever to enjoy the cool girl look.

What to Keep in Mind

Color

We’re not going to pretend that black isn’t the most versatile capsule wardrobe staple. However, that shouldn’t stop you from opting for some fun colors like red (just look at the Naked Wardrobe Faux The Love of Bootcut Pants) Other neutrals like brown, camel, forest green, or cream are great options to opt for year-round as well.

Material

Faux leather should mimic real leather in every way, especially in its feel, breathability, and durability. It’s usually made from polyurethane (PU) or polyvinyl chloride (PVC). The former is the more durable option of the two and shouldn’t crack and peel over time. To us, the higher price point for polyurethane-based faux leather is worth it for pants that will last longer and require less maintenance.

Fit and rise

Searching for our desired fit in pants comes with trial and error. With many styles to choose from, ranging from straight to bootcut to skinny, the right pair will flatter your figure, compliment your wardrobe, and provide enough comfort to wear throughout the day. But depending on your style preferences, some fits will offer you more or less room than others. Just as you would expect, relaxed pants are roomier around the waist and thigh, whereas slim pants are slimmer around the legs, but are not necessarily skinny. A boyfriend style, on the other hand, is much baggier in style than a relaxed pant, yet offers a straight design throughout the legs. You will also have to take into account the rise, as most pants are available in a low-rise, high-rise, super high-rise, and/or classic rise. If you want to have a safe and standard option, digital creator Sansha Coetzee recommends “high-waisted straight leg pants that crop at the ankle, because it’s the most flattering and versatile in terms of styling with shoes, like loafers, ballet flats, sneakers, heels, and ankle boots.” She also adds that this style won’t fall out of style and will remain a staple for decades to come. Your Questions, Answered

How do you wash faux leather pants?

Depending on the pair you own, most faux leather pants can be put in the washer on a cold cycle. Like genuine leather, faux leather requires extra care, so when you clean your pants, it is best to place them inside a machine-washable bag to prevent any damage. Another alternative to washing your faux leather pants is using a delicate, non-abrasive cloth dipped in water and a gentle soap to wipe over your pants. For both methods, allow your faux leather pants to hang dry or lay them flat to help maintain their shape and quality – never place faux leather in the dryer.

Do faux leather pants stretch?

Quality faux leather will fit snugly, never tight. But if you prefer a bit of stretch in your pants, there’s good news: faux leather will gradually stretch over time, especially in areas like the waist, knees, and legs. To quicken the process, fabric softener and warm water can always be used.

How do you style faux leather pants?

All the stylists we interviewed agreed on one thing: faux leather pants are extremely versatile. Ashton claims there are “endless ways to style faux leather pants, but one of my favorites for this fall is faux leather with a nice thick oversized cardigan with cute patent leather boots.” Coetzee chimes in that you can wear everything from “casual sneakers with a chunky sweater to kitten heels and an off-shoulder top for date night.” Meanwhile, wardrobe stylist Karlita Sutela recommends pairing them with pieces that “will totally oppose the smooth and embossed look and texture of leather pants — think rugged and edgy.” It’s true, we’ve found that there’s about nothing these pants can’t pull off. While jeans may be reserved for more casual outings, leather pants can be styled for the office just as well as for brunch. Just consider the specific style that you get, because certain options like the Cult Naked Killa Trousers are extra long and might benefit from heeled boots, while cropped pants like the Good American Good Legs Cropped Mini Boot Faux Leather Pants could easily work with sneakers.
